I wake and wander riverside<br>
on a crisp winter morning.<br>
Sunshine chases chills<br>
from my bones<br>
while my heart warms<br>
with wonder<br>
that I am welcome here<br>
in what will always be<br>
Aboriginal land.<br>
My history not white-washed<br>
but somehow forgiven.<br>
My love for this place<br>
a gift of grace.<br>

<i>Fiona H Evans has lived for 25 years in the suburb of Bayswater, by the Swan River in Perth. She walks the riverside every day in a place named Birralyn by local Noongar people long before it became Bayswater. Ever changing, ever the same, this place is as beautiful as it was 25 years ago. Probably as beautiful as it was a hundred years ago. Or 45,000 years ago, which is how long we have evidence of Noongar people living here. But of course, they’ve been here longer. This was always their land.</i>
